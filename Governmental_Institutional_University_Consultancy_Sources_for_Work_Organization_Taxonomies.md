# Governmental, Institutional, University and Consultancy Sources for Work Organization Taxonomies

## Overview: BambooHR Reference for HR Data Structure
**This document** integrates best practices with standardized international taxonomies, providing a solid foundation for complete HR systems and advanced analytics and provides a comprehensive reference model for organizing human resources data through its architecture based on customizable tables.

The platform organizes data into main categories such as:[^1_1][^1_2]

- **Employee Records**: personal information, emergency contacts, contract details, work responsibilities, salaries
- **Hiring & Recruitment**: candidate management, job postings, application tracking
- **Time & Attendance**: leave, attendance, work hours
- **Performance & Goals**: evaluations, objectives, skills development
- **Assets & Documents**: assigned equipment, signed documents, workflows
- **Custom Fields & Tables**: customizable fields for specific organizational needs[^1_3][^1_1]


## United States: Governmental and Institutional Sources

### O*NET (Occupational Information Network) - U.S. Department of Labor

**O*NET** represents the primary national source for occupational information in the United States:[^1_4][^1_5][^1_6]

- **Structure**: 923 data-level occupations organized into 867 SOC codes, grouped into 459 Broad Occupations, 98 Minor Groups and 23 Major Groups
- **Content**: knowledge, skills, abilities required for each role, training and education requirements, alternative titles
- **Updates**: database updated quarterly with main release in the third quarter
- **Applications**: job classification, career planning, workforce analysis[^1_7]


### Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) - OEWS Program

The **Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics** program provides:[^1_8][^1_9][^1_10]

- **Coverage**: annual estimates of employment and wages for approximately 830 occupations
- **Methodology**: semi-annual survey of 1.1 million establishments
- **Geographic granularity**: national, state, metropolitan and non-metropolitan area data
- **Occupational projections**: employment growth, separations, skill demand trends[^1_10]


### Standard Occupational Classification (SOC)

The **SOC 2018** system classifies:[^1_11]

- 867 detailed occupations grouped into 459 broad occupations
- 98 minor groups and 23 major groups
- Federal statistical framework for collection and dissemination of occupational data[^1_11]


## Europe: Institutional Frameworks

### ESCO (European Skills, Competences, Qualifications and Occupations)

**ESCO** provides the European multilingual classification with:[^1_12][^1_13][^1_14]

- **Structure**: 3,000+ occupations, 13,500+ knowledge/skills/abilities concepts
- **Languages**: available in 25 European languages
- **ISCO Mapping**: each ESCO occupation is mapped to an ISCO-08 code
- **Applications**: job-candidate matching, labor mobility, skills intelligence[^1_15][^1_16]


### CEDEFOP (European Centre for Development of Vocational Training)

**CEDEFOP** develops:[^1_17][^1_18][^1_19]

- **European Skills Index (ESI)**: composite indicator measuring performance of national skills systems
- **Skills Intelligence Tools**: supply/demand skills analysis, future skills forecasts
- **European Skills Panorama**: information on skills required by the labor market[^1_20][^1_17]


### Eurostat - Labour Force Survey (EU-LFS)

The **EU-LFS** covers:[^1_21][^1_22][^1_23]

- 34 European countries with data on employment, unemployment, work characteristics
- Common classifications: NACE, ISCO, ISCED, NUTS
- Annual ad-hoc modules on specific themes (work-family reconciliation, youth in the labor market)[^1_22]


### European Labour Authority (ELA)

**ELA** coordinates:[^1_24][^1_25][^1_26]

- Cross-border labor mobility
- Annual reports on occupational shortages and surpluses in Europe
- Data on posted workers, international road transport[^1_26][^1_27]


## Italy: National Sources

### ISTAT - Classification of Occupations

**CP2021** represents the Italian classification of occupations:[^1_28]

- 813 professional units based on ISCO logic
- Classification criteria: skill level and specialization
- Cross-linkable with international ISCO[^1_28]


### ANPAL (National Agency for Active Labor Policies)

**ANPAL** coordinates:[^1_29][^1_30][^1_31]

- National network of employment services
- Labor market information system
- Active labor policies and supply-demand matching[^1_29]


### INAPP (National Institute for Analysis of Public Policies)

The **Sample Survey on Occupations (ICP)** provides:[^1_32]

- Detailed data on tasks, skills, abilities, knowledge for all Italian occupations at 5 digits
- Only European source comparable with U.S. O*NET
- 797 occupational codes with survey of 16,000 Italian workers[^1_33][^1_32]


## International Frameworks

### ILO - International Standard Classification of Occupations (ISCO)

**ISCO-08** provides:[^1_34][^1_35][^1_36][^1_37]

- 436 unit groups organized in 4-level hierarchical structure
- 130 minor groups, 43 sub-major groups, 10 major groups
- Skill level (4 levels) and skill specialization as classification criteria
- Global framework for international occupational communication[^1_37][^1_38]


### World Economic Forum - Global Skills Taxonomy

The **WEF Global Skills Taxonomy** offers:[^1_39]

- Common global language for skills
- Framework for forecasting and benchmarking skill needs
- Support for Future of Jobs Report for emerging skills identification
- Toolkit for adoption by business, governments, education sector[^1_39]


## Other International Reference Sources

### United Kingdom

**UK Skills Taxonomy**:[^1_40][^1_41][^1_42]

- Data-driven taxonomy developed by Nesta with ~7,000 skills
- 3 layers: 6 broad clusters, 35 intermediate groups, 143 specific clusters
- Based on machine learning analysis of job advertisements[^1_42][^1_43]


### Australia

**Australian Skills Classification**:[^1_44][^1_45][^1_46]

- Framework based on ANZSCO with 3 categories: Core Competencies, Specialist Tasks, Technology Tools
- 600 occupational profiles with 10-point skill scales
- Managed by Jobs and Skills Australia[^1_46]


### Canada

**National Occupational Classification (NOC)**:[^1_47][^1_48][^1_49][^1_50]

- TEER framework (Training, Education, Experience, Responsibilities) with 6 categories
- 900 occupational profiles in the OaSIS system (Occupational and Skills Information System)
- Skills Match tool for identifying related occupations[^1_48]


### Lightcast (Ex-Burning Glass)

**Lightcast Skills Taxonomy**:[^1_51][^1_52][^1_53]

- 33,000+ skills from hundreds of millions of online job postings
- Bi-weekly updates with free API available
- Skill categories and subcategories for hierarchical organization
- Used by 6,000+ organizations globally[^1_51]


## Consultancy Sources

### McKinsey Global Institute

**Future of Work Model**:[^1_54][^1_55][^1_56]

- 25 workforce skills categorized into 5 groups: physical/manual, basic cognitive, higher cognitive, social/emotional, technological
- Skill demand projections to 2030
- Framework for reskilling and upskilling strategies[^1_56]


### World Bank

**STEP Skills Measurement Surveys**:[^1_57][^1_58]

- Skills development data for reducing underemployment
- Occupational trends and task content analysis
- LinkedIn talent migration data[^1_59][^1_60]


## Recommended Tables for Complete System

Based on the BambooHR model and identified sources, essential tables for a complete system should include:

**Core Entities**:

- Employees (personal data, contacts, documents)
- Positions/Jobs (descriptions, requirements, required skills)
- Departments/Units (organizational structure)
- Locations (sites, geographical data)

**Skills & Competencies**:

- Skills taxonomy (id, name, description, category, level)
- Employee_Skills (employee-skills mapping with levels)
- Position_Skills (skills required for positions)
- Skill_Categories (hierarchical organization of skills)

**Career Development**:

- Career_Paths (professional development paths)
- Learning_Programs (courses, certifications, training)
- Employee_Learning (employee training tracking)
- Performance_Reviews (evaluations, objectives)

**Workforce Analytics**:

- Employment_History (position history)
- Salary_Bands (salary ranges for roles)
- Market_Data (external salary/skills benchmarks)
- Succession_Planning (succession planning)[^1_2][^1_1][^1_3]

This structure integrates BambooHR best practices with standardized international taxonomies, providing a solid foundation for complete HR systems and advanced analytics.
